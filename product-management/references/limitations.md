# v1 Scope Exclusions & Limitations

This reference document outlines deliberate scope exclusions for the v1 release of the `product-management` agent skill. If your task falls into these categories, refer to the guidance below.

---

## 1. Go-To-Market (GTM) & Pricing Strategy
*   **Status:** Out of Scope for v1.
*   **Description:** This skill focuses heavily on product discovery cycles (Torres), outcome brief structuring (Cagan), and shaped cycle delivery (Singer). It does not contain specialized tools or references for defining GTM execution models, sales enablement materials, sales pricing/tier matrices, or monetization triggers.
*   **Workaround:** Supplement this skill with monetization-focused resources (e.g., Reforge monetization program, Lenny's Newsletter monetization deep-dives).
*   **Future Roadmap:** Candidate future sibling skill: `pm-gtm-pricing`.

---

## 2. Technical Strategy & Platform Trade-offs
*   **Status:** Out of Scope for v1.
*   **Description:** Beyond Marty Cagan's risk triage (Feasibility Risk Spikes), this skill does not contain architectural frameworks. It will not advise on database sharding, API versioning deprecation strategies, microservices migrations, or cloud infrastructure costing models.
*   **Workaround:** Consult with senior technical leads and refer to engineering strategy resources.
*   **Future Roadmap:** Candidate future sibling skill: `pm-technical-strategy`.

---

## 3. Consumer Growth Loops & Non-SaaS Models
*   **Status:** Out of Scope for v1.
*   **Description:** The frameworks contained in this skill (e.g., the Mom Test, Shape Up cycles, Marty Cagan briefs, and GEM sequencing) are optimized for B2B SaaS and utility products. It does not contain models for viral coefficients (k-factor), marketplace liquidity algorithms, consumer growth loops, or physical hardware manufacturing lifecycles.
*   **Workaround:** For consumer or hardware products, supplement with growth-loop frameworks (e.g., Reforge growth series).
*   **Future Roadmap:** Candidate future sibling skill: `pm-consumer-growth`.
