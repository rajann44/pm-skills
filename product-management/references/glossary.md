## 1. Strategy Terms

*   **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**
    *   *Definition:* The categorical definition of the target customer segment that experiences the highest problem intensity and represents the most profitable sales target.
    *   *Best Source:* Gibson Biddle / Reforge.
*   **DHM Model**
    *   *Definition:* A strategic filter checking if a product idea Delights customers, builds a Hard-to-copy competitive advantage, and improves business Margin.
    *   *Best Source:* Gibson Biddle.
*   **GLEe Model**
    *   *Definition:* A phased roadmapping model mapping sequential growth: Get Big on a beachhead, Lead in that space, Expand into adjacencies.
    *   *Best Source:* Gibson Biddle.
*   **GEM Model**
    *   *Definition:* An organizational alignment tool forcing leadership to force-rank Growth, Engagement, and Monetization priorities.
    *   *Best Source:* Gibson Biddle.
*   **Aggregation Theory**
    *   *Definition:* The restructuring of value chains on the internet where the dominant players are those who aggregate user demand via a superior user experience, rather than those who control supply or distribution.
    *   *Best Source:* Ben Thompson (Stratechery).
*   **Platform vs. Product**
    *   *Definition:* A "Product" is a closed, end-to-end user solution, while a "Platform" is an infrastructure or developer ecosystem that enables third parties to build their own products and businesses on top of it.
    *   *Best Source:* Ben Thompson (Stratechery).
*   **Empowered Team**
    *   *Definition:* A cross-functional product team (PM, Designer, Engineers) given a target problem/outcome to solve (rather than a feature output) and held accountable for results.
    *   *Best Source:* Marty Cagan (Silicon Valley Product Group).

## 2. Discovery Terms

*   **Continuous Discovery**
    *   *Definition:* A weekly rhythm of customer interviews and assumption testing conducted by a product trio (PM, Designer, Tech Lead) to co-create solutions with users.
    *   *Best Source:* Teresa Torres (Product Talk).
*   **Opportunity Solution Tree (OST)**
    *   *Definition:* A visual map linking a single business outcome (the goal) to underlying customer opportunities (needs, pains, desires), potential solutions, and rapid experiments.
    *   *Best Source:* Teresa Torres (Product Talk).
*   **Assumption Mapping**
    *   *Definition:* The process of identifying and prioritizing the underlying leap-of-faith assumptions for a proposed solution across the four key risks (Value, Usability, Feasibility, Viability).
    *   *Best Source:* Teresa Torres & Marty Cagan.
*   **The Mom Test**
    *   *Definition:* A customer interviewing technique that extracts objective facts about past behavior rather than hypothetical opinions, guesses, or polite compliments about future product ideas.
    *   *Best Source:* Rob Fitzpatrick (*The Mom Test* book).
*   **Commitment & Advancement Signals**
    *   *Definition:* A method for validating user interest at the end of a discovery meeting by requiring the customer to invest a scarce resource (Time, Reputation, or Money) to advance to the next step.
    *   *Best Source:* Rob Fitzpatrick (*The Mom Test* book).
*   **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**
    *   *Definition:* The smallest possible build or experiment that allows a team to gather validated learning about customers with the least amount of effort.
    *   *Best Source:* Eric Ries (*The Lean Startup*) / Teresa Torres (Product Talk).
*   **JTBD (Jobs-To-Be-Done)**
    *   *Definition:* A framework focusing on the core progress a customer is trying to make in a given circumstance (the "job" they hire the product to do).
    *   *Best Source:* Clayton Christensen (Harvard Business School).

## 3. Execution & Delivery Terms

*   **Appetite**
    *   *Definition:* The fixed amount of time a team decides they are willing to spend to solve a problem (e.g., 2 weeks or 6 weeks) before designing the solution scope.
    *   *Best Source:* Ryan Singer (Basecamp / *Shape Up*).
*   **Shaping**
    *   *Definition:* Pre-development design work that defines a project's boundaries at a medium level of abstraction (using breadboards and fat marker sketches) so it is concrete enough to build but flexible enough for developer design.
    *   *Best Source:* Ryan Singer (Basecamp / *Shape Up*).
*   **Betting Table**
    *   *Definition:* A cycle planning meeting where leadership reviews shaped pitches and commits to them. Backlogs are eliminated: if a pitch is not chosen, it is discarded.
    *   *Best Source:* Ryan Singer (Basecamp / *Shape Up*).
*   **Circuit Breaker**
    *   *Definition:* A policy stating that if a project is not completed within its cycle timebox, it is canceled and the team is reassigned, preventing runaway development cycles.
    *   *Best Source:* Ryan Singer (Basecamp / *Shape Up*).
*   **Hill Chart**
    *   *Definition:* A project tracking chart that divides work into two halves: the "uphill" phase (high uncertainty, figuring it out) and the "downhill" phase (low uncertainty, execution).
    *   *Best Source:* Ryan Singer (Basecamp / *Shape Up*).
*   **Now/Next/Later Roadmap**
    *   *Definition:* A roadmap format that replaces rigid timeline dates with three prioritized time horizons based on certainty and strategic alignment.
    *   *Best Source:* Janna Bastow (ProdPad / Mind the Product).

## 4. Growth & Metrics Terms

*   **Instrumentation**
    *   *Definition:* The implementation of software tracking (telemetry) to record user sessions, clicks, and behaviors, enabling quantitative product analysis.
    *   *Best Source:* Reforge.
*   **North Star Metric (NSM)**
    *   *Definition:* The single key metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to its customers, serving as the primary driver for long-term business growth.
    *   *Best Source:* Amplitude / Lenny Rachitsky.
*   **AARRR (Pirate Metrics)**
    *   *Definition:* A linear product funnel framework mapping the user journey through Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, and Revenue.
    *   *Best Source:* Dave McClure (500 Startups).
*   **Growth Loop**
    *   *Definition:* A closed-loop system where the output of one step is directly reinvested as an input to the next step, creating a self-reinforcing growth flywheel.
    *   *Best Source:* Brian Balfour (Reforge).
*   **Retention Cohort Analysis**
    *   *Definition:* Grouping users by signup date and tracking active usage over time. A flattening curve parallel to the x-axis indicates a stable user base, serving as the primary signal of Product-Market Fit.
    *   *Best Source:* Brian Balfour (Reforge) / Lenny Rachitsky.
*   **Proxy Metric**
    *   *Definition:* A measurable, short-term leading indicator that correlates directly to a lagging, long-term business outcome.
    *   *Best Source:* Gibson Biddle (former VP Product at Netflix).
